Framework

The DFR Framework

The Digital First Responder Framework maps clinical emergency response to cybersecurity incident response — the same decision method, applied to a patient, a hospital network, or a connected device. Triage. Stabilize. Treat. Recover.

Book a Session View Services

Why this exists

28 years in clinical emergency medicine teaches you one thing before anything else: when something is failing, you don't wait for the full picture before you act. You triage. Cybersecurity incident response asks the exact same question under the exact same pressure — what's actually critical, what can wait, and what do you do in the next five minutes. The DFR Framework is that clinical instinct, made explicit and applied to healthcare cyber risk.

The four phases

01

Triage

Clinical → Cyber

In the ER: Rapid assessment the moment a patient arrives — what's life-threatening, what's urgent, what can wait.

In an incident: Rapid assessment of what's actually been touched, what's critical to patient safety and operations, and what can wait until the next phase.

02

Stabilize

Clinical → Cyber

In the ER: Stop the bleeding, secure the airway — buy time before definitive treatment begins.

In an incident: Contain the threat, isolate affected systems, stop lateral movement — buy time before full remediation.

03

Treat

Clinical → Cyber

In the ER: The actual intervention — treating the cause, not just the symptom.

In an incident: Remediation — removing the threat, patching the actual vulnerability, restoring trust in the system rather than just restoring uptime.

04

Recover

Clinical → Cyber

In the ER: Monitoring, follow-up care, preventing relapse.

In an incident: Post-incident review, hardening, and building the resilience that keeps this from happening the same way twice.

Where it applies

Healthcare Cybersecurity

The foundational use case — IoMT risk, medical device security, and clinical AI governance, triaged and treated the way a clinical team triages and treats a patient.

AI Red Team for Healthcare Growth Lane

Adversarial testing of clinical AI runs through the same four phases — what could fail, what needs to be contained before it reaches a patient, what gets fixed, and what gets hardened afterward.

Sports Technology & Wearable Security Secondary / Future

Same method, applied to athlete biometric data and wearable device risk.
